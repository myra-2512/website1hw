Pizza

Pizza, dish of Italian origin consisting of a flattened disk of bread dough topped with some combination of olive oil, oregano, tomato, olives, mozzarella or other cheese, and many other ingredients, baked quickly—usually, in a commercial setting, using a wood-fired oven heated to a very high temperature—and served hot.It’s late at night and you want to nibble on something good but don’t feel like cooking. That’s the perfect occasion to order the family a pizza. But there’s so much more to pizza than you’d think. There are many varieties found around the globe. Here are some of the most popular pizzas out there. Get inspired and maybe next time you’ll discover a new one to try. A crunchy and yet soft crust. A mouthful of sweet and slightly tangy, fresh tomato sauce. Gooey, rich, unctuous cheese. Toppings of choice. It’s pizza time! While we take pizza for granted, there are many ways to enjoy this great dish.




Pastries

Pastry, a dough of flour, water, and shortening that may be savory or sweetened. It is used as a base and covering for various baked dishes, such as pies, tarts, quiches, and pasties. Pastry is a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of dishes, from sweet to savory. It can be made with different types of flour, such as all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, or gluten-free flour, and can be flavored with herbs, spices, or cheese. Pastry can be baked in a variety of ways, including blind baking, par-baking, and fully baking. Pastry is often used in desserts, such as pies, tarts, and pastries, but it can also be used in savory dishes, such as quiches, pot pies, and empanadas. It can be filled with a variety of ingredients, such as fruits, vegetables, meats, cheeses, and sauces.
